A Ralph Moody Family Album

 

"My goal in writing is to leave a record of the rural way of life in this country during the early part of the twentieth century, and to point out the values of that era which I feel that we, as a people, are letting slip away from us."

Ralph Owen Moody

The above picture was taken in Littleton, Colorado, in front of one of the houses the family lived in.  The people are: (back) Charles Moody, or father; (middle) Muriel, Grace, Mary Emma (mother), and Charles Moody's cousin's wife; (front) Philip, Hal, and Ralph.

Notice the three boys are looking down. A few weeks before, a little black dog that they were all very fond of disappeared. Right at the time of this picture, however, the little black dog came around the corner of the house and the boys were all looking at him. Unfortunately, the dog disappeared again; possibly due to a neighbor who didn't like him tromping through his/her garden.  This picture was taken six months before Ralph's father died.
