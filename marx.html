Harpo Speaks!


by Harpo Marx, with Rowland Barber

Here are 475 pages from the quietest Marx Brother, documenting the family's journey from New York's upper east side through Vaudeville, Broadway and Hollywood. Below are some Marx in time, highlights of why you should not miss this book:
Not so high school Marx -- At 8 years old, Harpo was thrown out of school for the last time.
Bookie Marx -- find out how to make a Chico-proof watch.
TradeMarx -- discover the origin of the "gookie".
Marx on Music --  "confessions of a non-lady harpist," and the little known "bubble clarinet."
Marx of literary genius -- Our second grade drop out (literally) becomes friends with Alexander Woollcott and some of the greatest writers of the time and joins Algonquin Round Table.
Marx of a Sportsman -- Croquet, anyone?
A Marxist secret agent? -- Not exactly, but Harpo's 1933 tour of Russia is a spy thriller.
Murder, he wrote -- Harpo, sweet, gentle, innocent Harpo Marx? It was just a game to him...
Laugh Marx -- the Hollywood Round Table with Jack Benny, George Burns, Eddie Cantor and Groucho Marx.
MarxMan -- Life on a Harp Ranch, complete with wife and children.
Pinchie Winchie Harpo Marx! -- or how to get a banker to lend you money without security.
